## Research Tracking Notes

### 1. Monitor Semiconductor Alignment
The high degree of alignment between NVDA, TSM, and AMD suggests a sector-wide trend. Analysts should look for signal invalidation—specifically breaks in the Bullish trend alignment (MACD/EMA/SMA200)—as a primary exit or caution indicator in paper-trading simulations.

### 2. Watch the Energy-Inflation Feedback Loop
With headline CPI at 3.95% and energy prices acting as "plumbing pressure," any significant drop in crude inventories or disruptions in maritime lanes (specifically the Persian Gulf watch item) should be viewed as a precursor to potential inflationary spikes.

### 3. Evaluate Sector Rotation
Capital is currently favoring Technology and Energy while avoiding Financials and Real Estate. This indicates a preference for "speed" (momentum) and "essentials" (energy) over interest-rate-sensitive sectors like Real Estate.

### 4. Utilize the "System View" for Accountability
Research should not be treated as a one-off stock pick. The "System View" requires tracking signal changes over time, variant views (what the market might be overestimating), and "priced-in checks" to determine if a move has already been exhausted by consensus.

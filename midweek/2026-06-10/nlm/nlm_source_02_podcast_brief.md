# Hive Weekly Podcast Brief - Week of Midweek Brief 2026-06-10

## Job Of The Episode

Make a dense market week sound like two sharp people explaining a complicated restaurant bill after dinner: clear, practical, lightly witty, and easy to follow. The podcast should be worth listening to on its own, but it should not replace the blog.

Target length: 30 to 40 minutes when the source packet is deep enough. Keep the energy conversational; do not pad. If the source material is lighter, a cleaner 25 to 32 minute episode is better than filler.

## Tone

- Funny, fun, conversational, and educational.
- Smart but approachable: everyday analogies, no gatekeeping, no meme energy.
- Layman-friendly: every finance term gets a one-sentence plain-English translation.
- Punchy: every segment needs a so-what, not just a data point.
- Keep the listener oriented: say what changed, why it matters, and what Hive watches next.
- Always say Hive. Avoid generic system labels.

## Segment Arc

1. Required short paper-trading disclaimer, then a fast hook.
2. What Hive Saw: the broad market read and the week's tension.
3. The System View: explain that Hive tracks a market dashboard over time, not one magic ticker call.
4. The Signal Chain: how macro, sector movement, and a few representative stock signals connect.
5. What Hive Did: describe paper-portfolio posture at a high level only.
6. What Hive Avoided: risks or crowded/fragile areas Hive did not chase.
7. The Risk Hive Is Watching: energy, maritime, credit, inflation, or sentiment risk.
8. What Hive Is Watching Next: one clean listener takeaway and a blog CTA.

## Product Tease

The paid-worthy idea is not a one-off article. The product is the repeatable system view: weekly market board, what changed since the last run, what could confirm or break the read, risk notes, data-quality flags, paper-portfolio tracker, and accountability notes. Mention that these live in the written report; do not read them all on the podcast.

## Blog CTA Placement

- First CTA after the first main segment: the full tables and daily stock notes live at https://wr7tdknwz5-blip.github.io/hive-podcast/blog.
- Mid-show CTA after the system view: listeners who want the market board, signal changes, priced-in checks, risk stacks, and breakpoints should read the weekly report.
- Closing CTA: daily single-stock breakdowns, the full weekly market board, and accountability notes are on Hive's written research home.

Good phrasing: 'The podcast is the map. The blog is where Hive keeps the receipts.'
Avoid phrases that sound like an institutional research terminal. Say the simple point first, then the data.

## Do Not Cannibalize The Blog

- Do not read full tables.
- Do not summarize every daily deep dive.
- Do not reveal all upgrades, downgrades, breakpoints, or the full Hive accountability log.
- Do not give the complete signal chain with every supporting data point.
- Do not make the listener feel they got the entire written product for free.
- Say that the full tables, charts, daily notes, and deeper risk work are reserved for the blog.
